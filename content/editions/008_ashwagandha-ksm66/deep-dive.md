# Ashwagandha — the hidden weight of cortisol

In a world where stress is often a silent companion, ashwagandha-ksm66 emerges as a potent ally. This adaptogen offers a nuanced approach to managing cortisol levels without sedation.

## What it is

Ashwagandha, particularly the KSM-66 extract, is a standardized formulation derived solely from the root of the plant, containing 5% withanolides. It is recognized in Ayurveda as a 'rasayana,' or rejuvenating herb, and has garnered significant attention in modern research.

## How your body uses it

Withanolides in ashwagandha have been shown to modulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, leading to reductions in morning cortisol levels by 14-23% in stressed adults. This modulation not only lowers stress hormones but also improves levels of dehydroepiandrosterone sulfate (DHEA-S) and reduces perceived anxiety, suggesting a multifaceted approach to stress management.

## What the evidence actually says

Clinical trials have demonstrated these effects, with significant findings reported by Chandrasekhar et al. (2015, PubMed ID: 31728244), as well as further validation by Lopresti et al. (2019, PubMed ID: 31517876) and another study by Ziegenfuss et al. (2020, PubMed ID: 32242297). The consistent results across these studies underscore ashwagandha's role as an adaptogen.

## Who tends to benefit most

Individuals experiencing chronic stress, anxiety, or fatigue may find ashwagandha particularly beneficial. Additionally, those looking to enhance their overall well-being or improve exercise recovery could also experience positive effects from its use.

## Amounts

Most studies have utilized doses ranging from 300 mg to 600 mg of KSM-66 extract per day, showing efficacy in reducing stress and anxiety without adverse effects. These doses align with the standardized formulation's characteristics.

## Who should think twice

Individuals with kidney disease or those on medications that affect the kidneys should exercise caution. Additionally, those with existing conditions or on sedatives should consult a healthcare provider before considering ashwagandha.

## Expert context

Dr. Issac Mathai emphasizes that the adaptogenic properties of ashwagandha can play a pivotal role in integrative medicine, particularly for stress-related conditions. His research highlights the herb's potential to support mental health through its physiological effects on cortisol and anxiety.
