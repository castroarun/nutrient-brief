# Omega-3 EPA & DHA — Building Blocks of Cellular Integrity

Omega-3 fatty acids, particularly EPA and DHA, are critical to maintaining cellular health. Yet, many Indian diets fail to provide adequate amounts of these essential fats.

## What it is

Eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA) are long-chain omega-3 fatty acids primarily found in marine sources. These fatty acids are crucial for cellular structure and function, particularly in the brain and heart.

## How your body uses it

EPA and DHA integrate into cell membranes, displacing arachidonic acid, which is associated with inflammatory processes. This displacement leads to a reduction in triglycerides through decreased hepatic very-low-density lipoprotein (VLDL) secretion. Furthermore, downstream metabolites such as resolvins and protectins play a role in mitigating vascular inflammation, enhancing cardiovascular health.

## What the evidence actually says

Research indicates that omega-3 supplementation can significantly lower triglyceride levels (Miller et al., 2018, PMID: 30415628) and improve inflammatory markers (Bistrian et al., 2018, PMID: 30019766). A meta-analysis also supports the cardiovascular benefits of these fatty acids (Bae et al., 2019, PMID: 31151903), while dietary patterns in India show a marked deficiency in EPA and DHA (Rao et al., 2012, PMID: 23076953).

## Who tends to benefit most

Sub-populations that may particularly benefit from EPA and DHA include individuals with elevated triglyceride levels, those at risk for cardiovascular disease, and vegetarians who lack direct sources of these fatty acids in their diets. Additionally, pregnant women may require adequate omega-3 intake for fetal brain development.

## Amounts

Clinical trials suggest that doses of EPA and DHA ranging from 1 to 4 grams per day may be effective in lowering triglycerides and improving heart health. However, the optimal dosage can vary based on individual health status and dietary intake.

## Who should think twice

Individuals with certain conditions, such as severe kidney disease or those on anticoagulant medications, should exercise caution with omega-3 supplementation due to potential interactions and increased bleeding risk.

## Expert context

Dr. Naresh Trehan emphasizes the importance of omega-3 fatty acids in cardiovascular health, noting that their anti-inflammatory properties can significantly impact heart disease outcomes. He advocates for increased awareness and dietary adjustments to incorporate these essential fats, especially in populations with limited access to marine sources.
