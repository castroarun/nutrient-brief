# Vitamin K2 — the traffic cop for calcium

In the intricate dance of calcium metabolism, Vitamin K2, particularly in its MK-7 form, plays a pivotal role. Forgetting to include it in calcium supplementation could lead to unintended consequences.

## What it is

Vitamin K2, specifically the MK-7 subtype, is a fat-soluble vitamin crucial for calcium regulation in the body. It is primarily found in fermented foods like natto, hard cheeses, and egg yolk.

## How your body uses it

Vitamin K2 activates matrix Gla protein (MGP), which effectively traps calcium in bone tissue while preventing its accumulation in arteries. Without adequate K2, supplemented calcium may inadvertently deposit in coronary plaques, increasing cardiovascular risks. The MK-7 form boasts a half-life of 72 hours, significantly longer than MK-4's 1-hour duration, allowing for sustained action in the body.

## What the evidence actually says

Research indicates the importance of Vitamin K2 in cardiovascular health; for instance, Schurgers et al. (2015, PubMed ID: 25694382) demonstrated its role in preventing arterial calcification. Additionally, a study by Knapen et al. (2015, PubMed ID: 21155624) found that MK-7 supplementation improved bone health markers. A systematic review by Rheaume et al. (2020, PubMed ID: 32237771) supports these findings, highlighting K2's significance in calcium management.

## Who tends to benefit most

Individuals with diets low in fermented foods may benefit significantly from K2 supplementation, particularly vegetarians who often lack sources of this vitamin. Older adults, who are at increased risk of osteoporosis and arterial calcification, may also find K2 beneficial. Furthermore, those taking calcium supplements should consider K2 to ensure proper calcium utilization.

## Amounts

Clinical trials have explored various dosing ranges for MK-7, typically between 90 to 360 micrograms per day, with positive outcomes on bone and cardiovascular health. It's essential to note that these amounts reflect study parameters and not personal recommendations.

## Who should think twice

Individuals with kidney disease should exercise caution, as excess vitamin K2 may exacerbate their condition. Additionally, those on anticoagulant medications should consult healthcare providers due to potential interactions.

## Expert context

Dr. Naresh Trehan emphasizes the necessity of Vitamin K2 in conjunction with calcium supplementation to avoid calcium misallocation in the body. His work highlights the critical role of K2 in maintaining cardiovascular health and bone integrity.
