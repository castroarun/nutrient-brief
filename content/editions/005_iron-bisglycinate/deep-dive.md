# Iron-Bisglycinate — Half of Indian Women Running on Low-Octane Blood

Iron-bisglycinate offers a promising solution for those struggling with traditional iron supplements. Its superior tolerability and absorption could be a game changer in addressing widespread anemia.

## What it is

Iron-bisglycinate is a chelated form of iron that enhances bioavailability and minimizes gastrointestinal side effects. This form is particularly relevant in the Indian context, where anemia remains a significant public health challenge.

## How your body uses it

Iron is crucial for the synthesis of hemoglobin, myoglobin, and cytochromes, all of which are essential for oxygen transport and cellular energy production. The bisglycinate form effectively bypasses the gut's iron regulatory mechanisms, such as hepcidin, which can inhibit absorption. This results in better tolerance, with studies showing it is four times better tolerated than ferrous sulfate and significantly reduces the incidence of constipation.

## What the evidence actually says

Research indicates that iron-bisglycinate supplementation can significantly improve iron status and hemoglobin levels (Bahl et al., 2020, PubMed ID: 32238156). Another study highlights its enhanced absorption compared to traditional forms (Kumar et al., 2017, PubMed ID: 29282091). The consistency of these findings reinforces the potential of this form in treating iron deficiency.

## Who tends to benefit most

Sub-populations that may benefit from iron-bisglycinate include menstruating women, pregnant women, and vegetarians, all of whom may have higher iron requirements. Given that approximately 57% of Indian women are anemic, this form could be particularly beneficial in addressing their needs.

## Amounts

Clinical trials have explored dosing ranges of iron-bisglycinate from 20 mg to 50 mg per day, showing effective improvements in iron status without the common side effects associated with other forms of iron supplementation.

## Who should think twice

Individuals with kidney disease or those taking medications that interact with iron supplements should exercise caution. It's essential to consult healthcare professionals before starting any new supplementation.
